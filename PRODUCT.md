# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

web

## Users

Four people in one family — Pithya (Danny), Shy, Bex, and Amma — planning and running a ten-day August 2026 trip from Muir Lake, Texas out to Las Vegas and back, with stops at the family land in Valle, Flagstaff, and Sedona. They use it on their phones far more than on a laptop: at the kitchen table arguing drive-vs-fly, and later on the road logging what they actually spent. Amma is older and has dietary needs the trip plans around. Everyone can edit, vote, and log — there is no admin.

## Product Purpose

One shared place to decide how the family gets to Vegas (drive the Forester, rent an SUV, fly, or split it with Amma flying while the others drive), see the trip laid out day by day, and keep the money honest — projected now, actual once real money moves. Success is the family reaching one locked-in plan they all voted on, and nobody surprised by the budget.

## Positioning

A trip planner that feels like it was made by this family for this trip — the real drive, the real land, the real Backstreet Boys night — not a generic travel dashboard.

## Brand Personality

Warm, playful, and personal, with a desert-to-neon sense of occasion. It talks like a family member who is excited about the trip, not like a SaaS app: "The big haul west," "Bex's solo night." Confident and specific over cute. The mood travels from desert dawn (warm sand, sunset orange) into Vegas night (neon pink and teal) as the trip does.

## Anti-references

Generic AI admin panels and SaaS dashboards. Emoji used as interface icons. Tiny uppercase tracked eyebrows above every section. Identical card grids. Anything that reads as "a template with this trip's data poured in." Muted light-gray body text for elegance.

## Design Principles

Make it feel handmade for this family — specific names, real quotes, real places, never placeholder voice. Money is honest: everything is projected until someone spends or books, and the interface never implies otherwise. Mobile-first, because most of this happens on a phone. Motion and neon are earned flourishes that mark the desert-to-Vegas arc, not decoration on every element. Everyone is a peer — edit, vote, and log are open to all four, with two-tap confirms guarding the destructive moves.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Dark neon surface, so contrast is watched closely — body and money text stay toward the ink end, not muted gray on tint. Every icon that carries meaning gets a label. Motion respects `prefers-reduced-motion`. Touch targets sized for phone use.
